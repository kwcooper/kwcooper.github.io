The term sonder is defined as the realization that each random passerby is living a life as vivid and complex as your own. It's one of my favorite ideas and it adds an interesting prospective. Just as you can't capture all that you are in an elevator pitch or biography, neither can I. But nevertheless, I'll have a go:

My name is Keiland Cooper, and in a nutshell, my goal in life is to make the short time we all currently have improve for as many people as possible; whether by research, technology, philosophy, or policy- if I have to leave this world, I want it to be a better place than when I arrived.

Currently, I attend Indiana University, with a focus in neuroscience and cognitive science. As a member of the CCNP lab, I conduct research a team of amazing people who aim to characterize the functional circuit-level processes of memory. This work directly supports where I am heading in the future, to understand human memory, from circuits to psychology, in an effort to repair and enhance the phenomenon. In addition, I write software, for research, business, and pleasure. ALso, I am passionate about education, and sit on my university's education policy committee and faculty council.
